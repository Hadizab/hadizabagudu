About the Author

Writing is an act of listening to the silence between words

Elena Whitmore is the New York Times bestselling author of twelve novels, including the National Book Award winner "The Silent Hours." Her work has been translated into twenty languages and adapted for both film and television.

She grew up in the Pacific Northwest, surrounded by tall pines and persistent rain. It was here, in the quiet moments between storms, that she discovered her love for storytelling. She currently divides her time between New York City and a small cottage in coastal Maine.

Early Life & Education

Born in 1978 in Portland, Oregon, Elena was the youngest of four children. Her mother was a librarian who instilled in her a love of books from an early age. She spent her childhood reading everything from Austen to Zola, often hiding in the library stacks during recess.

She attended Reed College, where she studied English Literature and Creative Writing, graduating summa cum laude in 2000. Her undergraduate thesis, a collection of short stories, won the college's prestigious fiction prize.

Career Beginnings

After college, Elena moved to New York City with $400 in her pocket and a manuscript in her backpack. She worked as a barista, a bookstore clerk, and a copy editor while writing in the early morning hours. Her first novel, "The First Snow," was rejected by 47 agents before finding a home with a small independent press.

That debut novel, published in 2008, sold only 3,000 copies in its first year. But a glowing review in the New York Times Book Review changed everything. Within months, it was optioned for film and went into its fifth printing.

Literary Success

Her breakthrough came with "The Light Between" (2018), which spent 32 weeks on the New York Times bestseller list and was selected for Oprah's Book Club. The novel has sold over two million copies worldwide.

"The Silent Hours" (2024) marked her arrival as a major literary voice, winning the National Book Award for Fiction and the Pulitzer Prize for Fiction. The novel is currently being adapted into a limited series starring Frances McDormand.

Teaching & Mentorship

Since 2015, Elena has taught creative writing at Columbia University School of the Arts, where she holds the position of Distinguished Writer-in-Residence. She is known for her intensive workshops on character development and narrative structure.

She also runs a scholarship program for emerging writers from underrepresented backgrounds, providing full funding for MFA candidates at three universities.

Personal Life

Elena lives in Brooklyn Heights with her partner, photographer Sarah Chen, and their two rescue dogs, Virginia and Leonard. She is an avid collector of vintage typewriters and first edition books.

When not writing, she practices yoga, experiments with sourdough baking, and walks the Brooklyn Bridge at dawn. She is currently at work on her thirteenth novel, set in 1960s Sicily.

Media & Adaptations

Her work has been adapted for film and television multiple times. "Winter's Edge" became a critically acclaimed HBO miniseries starring Jessica Chastain. "The Glass House" is currently in development with A24 Films.

Elena has appeared on NPR's Fresh Air, The Late Show with Stephen Colbert, and has been profiled in The New Yorker, Vanity Fair, and Vogue.
